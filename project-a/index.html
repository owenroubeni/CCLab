Jellyfluxfish

Scientific Name:

Fluxaris-7

Discovered by / at:

Marine biologists in a Yellow Submarine

Family:

This jellyfish belongs to the Chromomedusa species.

Habitat:

The Deep Trenches of the Ocean

Appearance:

A normal jellyfish appearance with a blue-green luminescence at first, but when the light turns on and trash is surrounding, it shrinks and changes color depending on its surroundings. It may or may not also start defying the laws of Jellyfish motion.

Key Characteristics:

  • Does not like bright light
  • Changes from blue to green hues in deep dark water
  • Overwhelmed by ocean pollution
  • Isolated

What we know so far about the Jellyfluxfish The Jellyfluxfish (Chromomedusa Fluxaris-7) is a rare, color-shifting jellyfish that reacts to light and pollution, glowing blue-green in the dark and turning pink in bright conditions. It tends to live in isolation, rarely found with others of its kind. Marine biologists study its bioluminescence and adaptability to understand the condition of the ocean.
Guidelines for human interaction with this creature Given you are a scientist in a Yellow Submarine, there is no way to directly interact with the Jellyfluxfish. Instead, observing the creature from the inside of the Submarine allows you to press a button. This button reveals the pollution in the ocean, making the Jellyfluxfish react. The trash also has an effect on color and movement.
